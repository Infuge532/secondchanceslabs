It started with a downed tree across a mountain road in Breckenridge — cut to length right there in the street, hauled out on a snow sled, and turned into a fireplace mantle. Thirty-three years of carpentry and general contracting later, that instinct became Second Chance Slabs.

The process

From fallen tree to finished table

01 · Sourcing

The hunt

The more unique the piece, the more it captures the eye. We look for quilted figure, branch inclusions, wild live edges — slabs with a story already in them.

02 · Drying & milling

Kiln and flatten

Up to two weeks in the kiln pulls the moisture out. Then the slab is CNC-milled dead flat, so it stays true for decades.

03 · Composition

Layout and design

Slabs are arranged inside the form until the composition is right — a maker's hands and an interior designer's eye on every piece.

04 · The pour

Seal and pour

A clear seal coat first, then deep-pour epoxy up to two inches at a time — temperature-controlled through its slow exothermic cure.

05 · Patience

The cure

Three days before the form comes off. Seven before the next stage. Good tables can't be hurried.

06 · Surfacing

Flatten and sand

Milled level again, then nearly a week of sanding through eight grits — sixty to one thousand — until wood and river read as one surface.

07 · Finishing

Protect for a lifetime

Ceramic-coated top, fully sealed underside, steel C-channels routed in against the seasons. Built to be handed down, not replaced.
