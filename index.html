Fallen Trees,
Given a Second Chance.

Every table begins as a walnut slab that once stood as a tree. We frame it, pour it, and sand it — until it's ready to live again as your family's heirloom.

Standing

A walnut grows for eighty years, recording every season in its grain.

Fallen

A storm, a road crew, a forgotten lot. Most of these trees become firewood.

Rescued

We find the ones worth saving — the quilted grain, the wild live edges, the character.

Heirloom

Months later it anchors a home or a boardroom, ready for its next hundred years.

It started with a downed tree across a mountain road in Breckenridge — cut to length right there in the street, hauled out on a snow sled, and turned into a fireplace mantle. Thirty-three years of carpentry and general contracting later, that instinct became Second Chance Slabs.

Inside the Craft

Built Slowly.
Built to Outlast Us.

No two slabs are alike — a branch that quilted the grain, an edge the forest distressed. We look for the character most mills throw away, then spend weeks bringing it to light.
