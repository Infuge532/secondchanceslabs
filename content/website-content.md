## 4. The Process (strongest storytelling section)

A clear, real 7-stage journey — perfect for a scroll-driven narrative:

1. **The Hunt** — sourcing unique slabs; "the more unique the piece, the more it captures somebody's eye." Looks for character: branch growth, quilting, live edges, distress
2. **Kiln & Flatten** — moisture removed in a kiln (up to 2 weeks), slab flattened by his CNC partner in Paola, KS
3. **Layout & Design** — slabs arranged inside a custom form on the floor; Nancy gives the final say on composition; marked in chalk, cut to fit
4. **Seal & Pour** — clear epoxy seal coat to prevent leaks, then Total Boat "Fathom" deep-pour epoxy (up to 2" per pour); exothermic cure managed at ~70°F shop temp
5. **Cure** — 3 days to strip the form, 7 days before the next stage
6. **Surface** — CNC-flattened dead level, then ~a week of hand sanding through 8 grits (60 → 1000)
7. **Finish & Protect** — ceramic finish system, C-channels routed underneath for seasonal stability, bottom fully sealed ("if you don't finish the bottom, the table's trash")

**Time investment (great trust/credibility numbers):**
- Coffee table: ~40 hours
- Dining table: 80–100 hours
- Large showpiece: ~150 hours
